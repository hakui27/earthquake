What is an Earthquake?

It's a sudden shaking of the ground caused by the passage of seismic waves through Earth’s rocks. Seismic waves are produced when some form of energy stored in Earth’s crust is suddenly released, usually when masses of rock straining against one another suddenly fracture and “slip.”

What to do Before

  • Make an evacuation plan and a meeting place so that everyone knows where to go.
  • Do evacuation drills regularly to help strengthen muscle memory for when an earthquake hits.
  • Prepare a first aid kit with daily neccesities so that you're prepared for whatever trouble you're facing.

    • What to do During

  • Duck, Cover, and Hold under a table or underneath the doorway if not nearby a table.
  • Then slowly evacuate going through the nearest route. Avoid running or talking.
  • Pay attention to the one in charge and take attendance, if someone is missing report them to the rescuers to try and find them.

    • What to do After

  • Stay away from any nearby structures, high trees, or anything capable of falling down. There is still a possibility for an aftershock to occur and could possibly injure you.
  • Call parents/guardians to make sure that both of you are safe from the earthquake.
  • After checking the situation, if the earthquake has subsided, the one in charge can dismiss all to return back home if it's done at school or at a workplace.
